Paneer Tikka Masala:


    Ingredients:

For the Paneer Tikka:


  • 250g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cut into cubes
    • 

  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt
    • 

  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 

  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 

  • Salt to taste
    • 

  • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 

For the Masala Sauce:


  • 2 tablespoons butter or oil
    • 

  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 

  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (for color, not too spicy)
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 

  • 1 cup tomato puree
    • 

  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
    • 

  • Salt to taste
    • 

  • Chopped cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish
    • 



    
Instructions:


  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, salt, and oil to make the marinade for the paneer.
    2. 

  2. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and let them marinate for about 30 minutes to an hour.
    3. 

  3. Preheat your oven to 200°C (390°F). Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto skewers and place them on a baking tray. You can also cook them on a stovetop
 grill or a non-stick pan if you don't have an oven.
    4. 

  4. Bake or grill the paneer cubes for about 15-20 minutes or until they are lightly charred and cooked through. If using a stovetop pan, cook until they
 are golden brown on all sides.
    5. 

  5. In a separate pan, heat butter or oil. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    6. 

  6. Add ginger and garlic paste, and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    7. 

  7. Add cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, paprika, and garam masala. Sauté for a minute.
    8. 

  8. Add tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
    9. 

  9. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer for a few minutes.
    10. 

  10. Season the masala sauce with salt and adjust the spices according to your taste.
    11. 

  11. Gently add the cooked paneer cubes to the masala sauce and let them simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
    12. 

  12. Garnish with chopped cilantro (coriander leaves).
    13. 

  13. Serve the Paneer Tikka Masala with naan, rice, or roti.
    14. 

  14. This dish is creamy, rich, and not overly spicy, making it suitable for picky eaters. Enjoy your cooking and your date!
    15.
