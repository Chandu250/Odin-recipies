Cashew Curry Recipe

Ingredients:

For the Cashew Paste:

    

  • 1 cup cashew nuts (kaju), soaked in warm water for 2 hours
    • 

  • 1/4 cup water (for grinding)
    • 



For the Curry:

    

  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 

  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 

  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
    • 

  •  tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 

  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • 

  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 

  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • 

  • Salt to taste
    • 

  • 1 cup water
    • 

  • 1/4 cup heavy cream or yogurt (optional, for creamines
    • s)

  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)


Instructions:

    
    Prepare Cashew Paste:
        

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and place them in a blender.
    2. 

  2. Add 1/4 cup of water and blend until you get a smooth paste. Set aside.

    3. 




    
Cook the Curry:


  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat.
    2. 

  2. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds until fragrant.
    3. 

  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent and golden brown.
    4. 




    
Add Spices:


  1. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    2. 

  2. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.

    3. 



    
Create the Cashew Base:


  1. Reduce the heat to low and add the cashew paste to the pan. Mix well to combine with the onion-tomato mixture.
    2. 

  2. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously, allowing the flavors to meld.
    3. 



    
Simmer and Add Liquid:


  1. Pour in 1 cup of water and bring the curry to a gentle simmer. You can adjust the amount of water based on your desired consistency.
    2. 

  2. Let the curry simmer for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.
    3. 



    
Finish the Curry:


  1. If using cream or yogurt, add it to the curry and mix well. This will add creaminess to the curry.
    2. 

  2. Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and stir it in.
    3. 

  3. Taste and adjust salt and spices as needed.
    4. 



    
Serve:


  1. Once the curry is well-cooked and has a thick consistency, remove it from the heat.
    2. 

  2. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro leaves.
    3. 


Enjoy:

Serve the cashew curry hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti. It's a delicious and indulgent dish that's perfect for special occasions or when you want to treat yourself.
Note: You can customize the level of spiciness and creaminess in this curry according to your taste preferences. Feel free to adjust the amount of chili powder and cream/yogurt accordingly.
